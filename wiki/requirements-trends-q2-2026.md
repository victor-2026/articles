## Trend 1: AI Artifact Governance

**Signal:** CluePoints, Morgan Stanley, TELUS

Companies are past the "try AI" phase. Now they ask:
- How do you review AI-generated code?
- How do you know it's reliable?
- What's your guardrail for false positives?

**What it means:** AGENTS.md + 6 gates + self-evaluation 4 axes = competitive advantage. Format this as "AI Artifact Review Process" in CV.

**Keywords to use:** `AI artifact governance`, `AI-generated code review`, `guardrails for AI output`, `human-in-the-loop validation`

---

## Trend 2: AI-First Testing (Not AI-Augmented)

**Signal:** CluePoints ("AI-first engineering mindset")

Difference matters:
- **AI-augmented** — occasional copilot use (95% of candidates)
- **AI-first** — embedded AI tools in every stage: test design, generation, execution, evaluation

**What it means:** You're in the 5% who actually ran controlled AI experiments. Lead with this.

**Keywords:** `AI-first testing`, `AI-assisted test generation`, `intelligent regression optimization`

---

## Trend 3: Release Readiness & Go/No-Go

**Signal:** CluePoints, Decskill, OneJar

Enterprise maturity signal. Companies want someone who can:
- Lead release readiness meetings
- Define Go/No-Go criteria
- Present quality metrics to executive stakeholders

**What it means:** You have this (DORA, quality gates) but use different vocabulary. Map your experience to their terms.

**Keywords:** `Release Readiness`, `Go/No-Go governance`, `release quality gates`, `structured quality metrics`

---

## Trend 4: Full-Stack Testing (API + UI + Data + Performance)

**Signal:** CluePoints, Decskill, Logifuture, OneJar

No more "UI tester" or "API tester" roles. Everyone wants full-stack:
- Backend APIs and services
- Frontend UI
- Data layers / event-driven
- Performance / non-functional

**What it means:** You're covered. Buzzhive = API + UI + DB + PBT + contract. OrangeHRM = UI + k6 + API. But make sure CV says "full-stack" explicitly.

**Keywords:** `full-stack testing`, `multi-layer validation`, `API + UI + data`, `end-to-end quality coverage`

---

## Trend 5: Hands-On Leadership (Player-Coach)

**Signal:** Every single JD reviewed

Every company wants both:
- Strategy and team management
- Ability to write tests and debug automation

Pure managers are out. Player-coach is in.

**What it means:** Your profile (built 0→9 + wrote 330+ tests) is exactly what the market wants. Lead with this duality.

**Keywords:** `player-coach model`, `hands-on leadership`, `strategy + execution`

---

## Trend 6: Risk-Based & Metrics-Driven

**Signal:** CluePoints, Decskill, BVP, OneJar

ISTQB-style risk-based testing is coming back, but now with data:
- DORA metrics
- Quality dashboards
- Risk registry with quantified impact

**What it means:** Your DORA + Grafana + Allure combination is rare. Most test managers talk about metrics, few actually build dashboards.

**Keywords:** `risk-based testing`, `DORA metrics`, `quality dashboards`, `data-driven quality decisions`

---

## Trend 7: Domain-Specific Testing Knowledge Less Demanded

**Signal:** CluePoints, Decskill, Logifuture, BVP

Across all JDs, **domain knowledge** is mentioned but almost never as "must-have":
- CluePoints: life sciences clinical trials — not in requirements
- Decskill: no domain specified
- Logifuture: iGaming — not in requirements
- BVP: No specific domain

Exception: Orient Path (cloud infra) and Morgan Stanley (GenAI) — but these are senior/director roles where domain becomes relevant.

**What it means:** Methodology transfer is real. Companies value quality leadership over domain experience for manager+ roles.
